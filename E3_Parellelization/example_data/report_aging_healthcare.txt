RESEARCH REPORT: Aging Populations and Healthcare Systems
Date: December 2024
Author: Dr. Isabella Romano, Gerontology and Public Health Institute

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This report examines demographic shifts toward aging populations and their implications for healthcare systems, social services, and economic structures.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Demographic Trends
- Global population aged 65+: 771 million (9.3% of world population)
- Projected to reach 1.5 billion by 2050 (16% of population)
- Life expectancy increasing 2.5 years per decade
- Fastest growth in 85+ age group

2. Healthcare System Impacts
- Chronic disease treatment accounts for 75-85% of healthcare costs
- Long-term care needs increasing 50% per decade
- Shortage of geriatric specialists projected at 50,000+ globally
- Mental health conditions affect 26% of older adults

3. Economic Implications
- Pension systems face sustainability challenges
- Workforce participation declining in developed countries
- Healthcare expenditure projected to double by 2050
- Long-term care costs exceed $1 trillion annually

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Redesign healthcare systems for chronic disease management
2. Invest in geriatric training and specialist development
3. Promote healthy aging and disease prevention
4. Support technology solutions for care delivery
5. Reform pension and social security systems
6. Encourage productive aging and workforce participation
7. Develop accessible housing and support services
8. Address social isolation and mental health needs

CHALLENGES
- Financing long-term care sustainability
- Workforce capacity and training gaps
- Social isolation in older populations
- Technology adoption barriers
- Geographic disparities in care access

CONCLUSION
Aging populations require comprehensive societal adaptations in healthcare, social services, and economic policy to ensure quality of life and system sustainability.
